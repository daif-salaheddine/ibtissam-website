01

The story behind the work

Ibtissam's career is not a linear climb. It is a deliberate journey across two continents, three languages, and multiple sectors, animated by one question: how do we design learning that actually changes how people think, work, and lead?

She began in Boston, working in Fisher College's President's Office, teaching international students, and conducting institutional research. After joining MassHire first as a client and experiencing the power of personalized coaching, her coach eventually became her supervisor, a full-circle moment that crystallized her calling. She founded Youngsters Stewards of the Earth, introducing children to environmental stewardship through experiential learning, and explored education policy through Washington D.C. and the World Bank, before returning to Morocco with a clear sense of purpose.

Returning during COVID-19, she joined Al Akhawayn University to explore liberal arts education in a Moroccan context, co-designing curriculum, mapping NECHE accreditation outcomes, and mentoring over 300 students.

Her intellectual roots run deeper than any institution. She draws from Al-Ghazali's holistic view of human development, Ibn 'Atā' Allah Al-Iskandarī's understanding of learning as ongoing transformation, and Rumi's invitation to follow what you genuinely love. For her, learning is everywhere, waiting to be engaged with.

"The goal is to become useless — to build something that can outgrow you. A culture that continues to choose well. A system that keeps serving people. A mission that remains clear even when your name is no longer attached to it."

How she thinks about learning

"Care is not an afterthought. It is a design principle — one that shapes participation, trust, and engagement. And maybe the real measure of impact is not how much happens because of us, but how much continues without us."

THE FOUR-STAGE METHODOLOGY
01

Discover

Before designing anything, she listens.

Context is not background information. It is the design brief.

→
02

Design

Programs built backward, from the outcome required.

Through the assessment that proves it, to the content that builds toward it.

→
03

Deliver

Facilitation that is alive, experiential, adaptive, and relational.

Not a slide deck read aloud, but a space where learning happens.

→
04

Evaluate & Transfer

The loop must close.

Outcomes measured, capability embedded, so results outlast her presence in the room.

BELIEFS THAT SHAPE EVERY ENGAGEMENT

Learning is everywhere

A classroom, a café conversation, a pottery studio — any space where minds meet is a learning space.

She designs with that expansiveness.

Rooted in tradition, open to the world

She draws from Al-Ghazali, Ibn 'Atā' Allah Al-Iskandarī, and Rumi as naturally as from liberal arts pedagogy.

Local wisdom and global frameworks enrich each other.

Human agency over acceleration

In the era of AI, leadership means asking: just because we can build it, should we?

She designs for human presence, consent, and intentionality.

Teaching is leadership

The most powerful moment in a room is when learners speak freely.

When they realize that much of what they need already exists within the group, that is when real learning begins.

Systems outlast individuals

The best engagement ends with the client needing her less.

Because structure, capability, and culture are now embedded in the organization itself.

We are students to some, teachers to others

Learning is never one-directional.

As much as you teach, you learn alongside those in the room. This is how she genuinely approaches every engagement.
